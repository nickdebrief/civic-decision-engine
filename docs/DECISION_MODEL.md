# Civic Decision Engine – Decision Model

The Civic Decision Engine analyses civic cases by modelling how
institutions, actors, evidence, and procedural timelines interact
within a decision environment.

The system is designed to help identify patterns in institutional
responses and evaluate when escalation or intervention may be appropriate.

The model is inspired by real-world civic complaint processes and
regulatory oversight structures.


## Core Principles

The engine evaluates civic cases using four analytical pillars:

• Evidence  
• Time  
• Procedure  
• Incentives  


### Evidence

Evidence represents the factual basis of a case.

Signals analysed include:

- documentation quality
- corroboration
- traceability
- evidential completeness

Higher evidence strength increases escalation readiness and reduces
uncertainty in the case analysis.


### Time

Time influences decision environments through deadlines and procedural windows.

Signals include:

- statutory deadlines
- response windows
- elapsed time since action
- proximity to escalation thresholds


### Procedure

Institutions follow procedural structures that shape outcomes.

The engine evaluates:

- procedural stage
- lifecycle stability
- response completeness
- procedural compliance


### Incentives

Actors within institutions respond to incentives and risk structures.

Signals include:

- reputational risk
- regulatory exposure
- internal escalation thresholds
- institutional workload pressures


## Decision Signals

The engine produces several analytical signals during case evaluation.

Examples include:

• Evidence Strength  
• Deadline Pressure  
• Pattern Signal  
• Escalation Readiness  
• Case Momentum  


These signals help interpret the posture of a civic case and
support strategic decision making.


## Escalation Logic

Escalation decisions are evaluated using multiple factors:

- jurisdiction relevance
- evidence strength
- procedural maturity
- deadline proximity
- potential institutional response


The engine produces an escalation ranking that indicates which
external review pathways may be appropriate.


## Lifecycle Diagnostics

Lifecycle diagnostics evaluate the stability of the current stage
within the civic case lifecycle.

Possible states include:

- stable observation
- preparation for escalation
- active escalation
- resolution monitoring


Diagnostics help determine whether a case should:

• remain in observation  
• continue internal engagement  
• escalate to oversight bodies  
• close and archive  


## Pattern Learning

The system is designed to capture patterns across multiple civic cases.

Over time, this allows analysis of:

- institutional behaviour patterns
- escalation success rates
- systemic delays
- structural decision dynamics


Future versions may incorporate portfolio-level analytics and
institutional network mapping.


## Research Direction

The Civic Decision Engine is an experimental civic technology model.

Future development may explore:

- institutional network analysis
- escalation outcome modelling
- case portfolio analytics
- decision environment simulation
