# Stage 39 — Meta-Framework Reflection

## Purpose

Stage 39 deterministically reflects on the Civic Record Evaluation Framework as
a methodology category. It describes what kind of framework CREF is, what it
contributes, how it remains distinct from implementation-specific software, and
how it intersects with adjacent methodological domains.

## Why Stage 39 Matters

Stage 36 established methodological self-containment, Stage 37 declared
stewardship responsibilities, and Stage 38 assembled the preservable legacy
package. Stage 39 uses those visible outputs to describe CREF's broader
methodological character without introducing new evaluation or external claims.

## Reflection Items

The complete fixture contains 33 deterministic reflection items:

- 27 reflection items documented directly;
- 6 reflection items documented with explicit limitations;
- 0 unavailable reflection items; and
- 0 meta-framework gaps.

The qualified items concern adjacent-domain relevance, cross-context
interpretability, and methodology portability. Their limitations prevent
internal reflection from becoming a claim of external validity, replacement,
adoption approval, or implementation correctness.

## Adjacent Domains

CREF intersects with public administration, record keeping, decision science,
and digital governance. These relationships describe relevance only. CREF does
not replace those disciplines, their standards, or their institutional roles.

## Relationships

Nine deterministic relationships describe methodology category,
implementation, evidence and governance boundaries, portability, adjacent
domains, the Stage 38 legacy package, the Stage 40 future boundary, and the
visible source of reflection.

- **Executive Report:** reflection overview and concise limitations.
- **Review Report:** overview, nine relationships, and full limitations.

Full Inspection remains the default, and Stage 39 follows Stage 38.

## Limitations

Meta-Framework Reflection is not legal analysis, academic peer review, or
external validation. It does not validate evidence, determine truth or
liability, create authority, infer future stages or undocumented
implementations, certify software portability or implementation correctness,
approve adoption, or modify any record or prior output.
