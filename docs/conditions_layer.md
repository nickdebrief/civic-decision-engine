Conditions Layer — Civic Decision Engine

Overview

The Conditions Layer defines a set of named, reusable institutional behaviour states identified across civic cases.

These are not scores or interpretations.

They are classified conditions that describe how a system is operating.

⸻

Purpose

The Conditions Layer allows the Civic Decision Engine to move from:
  • describing behaviour
to
  • diagnosing system state

Once a condition is identified, it can be:
  • named
  • cited
  • applied across cases
  • accumulated as part of a broader pattern

⸻

Principle

When a system can support named conditions, it stops describing behaviour and begins diagnosing it.

⸻

What is a Condition?

A condition is:
  • a defined state of institutional behaviour
  • identified through observable structural signals
  • repeatable across cases
  • independent of individual interpretation

⸻

Why Conditions Matter

Descriptions can be dismissed as interpretation.

Conditions require rebuttal on their own terms.

A description explains behaviour.
A named condition allows it to be recognised again.

⸻

Core Conditions (v1)

C001 — Stability Without Confirmation

Definition:
An institution continues to function without producing external signals that confirm resolution.

Diagnostic Question:
Does the system continue in the absence of external confirmation that the issue was resolved?

Detectable Signs:
  • administrative continuity without confirmed resolution
  • absence of follow-up despite claimed completion
  • internal closure signals without external proof of containment

Implication:
Operational continuity should not be mistaken for confirmed resolution.

⸻

C002 — Closure Without Containment

Definition:
A process is formally closed, but the underlying issue remains functionally unresolved in the real world.

Diagnostic Question:
Was the case closed administratively without the underlying issue being contained?

Detectable Signs:
  • formal closure notice issued
  • persistence of harm or unresolved issue
  • no traceable remedial change

Implication:
Administrative closure should not be mistaken for substantive resolution.

⸻

C003 — Process Completion Without Outcome

Definition:
Required procedural steps are completed, but those steps do not produce a meaningful or traceable outcome.

Diagnostic Question:
Did the institution complete the process without producing a substantive outcome?

Detectable Signs:
  • acknowledgement issued
  • review completed
  • procedural steps documented
  • no corrective or enforceable outcome

Implication:
Procedural activity should not be mistaken for substantive effect.

⸻

Relationship Between Conditions

Conditions are not mutually exclusive.

A single case may exhibit multiple conditions simultaneously.

Example:
  • Process Completion Without Outcome
→ followed by
  • Closure Without Containment
→ sustained as
  • Stability Without Confirmation

This layering allows the Engine to capture progression of institutional behaviour over time.

⸻

Application

Conditions are applied when:
  • the definition is met exactly
  • observable signals are present
  • the classification holds without reinterpretation

If a case does not meet the definition, the condition is not applied.

⸻

Important Distinction

Conditions are not:
  • scores
  • severity ratings
  • subjective impressions

They are:

diagnostic classifications of system state
