# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

The Civic Recall Pipeline project is an open and transparent effort to explore accountability, fairness, and auditability in civic information systems.

This repository welcomes constructive contributions, discussion, and collaboration from developers, researchers, and members of the public who share an interest in transparency, algorithmic accountability, and public-interest technology.

To ensure a respectful and productive environment, all participants are expected to follow this Code of Conduct.

---

## Expected Behaviour

Participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and considerate in communication.
- Engage in good-faith discussion.
- Support evidence-based reasoning and transparency.
- Focus on improving the quality, integrity, and clarity of the project.
- Respect differing viewpoints and constructive criticism.
- Contribute in ways that strengthen the reliability and usefulness of the project.

---

## Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviours are not acceptable in this project:

- Harassment, personal attacks, or abusive language.
- Discriminatory or hateful behaviour.
- Intentional misinformation or bad-faith manipulation.
- Disruptive behaviour that prevents productive collaboration.
- Attempts to misuse the project for harassment, political manipulation, or coordinated abuse.

---

## Responsible Participation

Because this project explores civic systems and public information processes, contributors are encouraged to act responsibly and with awareness of the potential real-world impacts of technology.

Contributions should aim to:

- improve transparency
- support accountability
- encourage responsible public dialogue

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove comments, issues, or contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

Repeated violations may result in restrictions on participation in the repository.

---

## Acknowledgment

By participating in this repository, contributors agree to follow this Code of Conduct and help maintain a respectful and constructive community environment.
